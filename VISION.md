# Tirzah Reader — Vision

## The Problem
Tong Sin wants to read English books but hits 20-40% unknown words, turning reading into decoding. Looking up words on her phone breaks flow and invites distraction. She defaults to Chinese because it's frictionless.

## The Insight
The science says you need to know 98% of words on a page to read for pleasure. Below 95%, comprehension collapses. The tool's job: bring effective coverage into the 95-98% band through intelligent glossing, so she can actually enjoy reading in English.

## The Solution
A mobile-first web reader where she can tap any word for a brief, level-calibrated definition. The system tracks what she knows and fades support as vocabulary builds. No separate app to install — just a URL on her phone.

## Design Philosophy
- Reading flow is sacred — never break it
- Definitions should be simpler than dictionary entries
- L1 (Chinese) support is scaffolding, not a crutch — it fades
- The best vocabulary review is encountering words again in context
- Track progress invisibly — she just reads, the system learns

## Target User
Tong Sin — intermediate English, native Chinese (Traditional), living in Canada, pregnant, motivated to read this specific parenting book.

## Success Metric
She finishes the book in English.
